Raw Electrical Activity at Electrodes

It can be helpful to watch the channel activity fluctuate through time to gain further insight into spatial relationships in the flow of electrical signals.

.

Electrode Channel Networks

To look more closely at which channels are co-dependent, we fit a dynamic gaussian graphical model which connects two nodes in the graph if they are conditionally dependent on eachother. This visualization displays which electrodes display similar voltages over each time bin, to better understand the flow of electrical signals.

Subject Network

Finally, we fit another dynamic gaussian graphical model to the data at the subject level, averaging over all channels. We might expect that alcoholic subjects are more related to alcoholics and controls to controls. This visualization shows us that at various time points we see patterns of activity that do not necessarily reflect this expectation. Red borders indicate alcoholic subjects, blue borders indicate control subjects. The color of each node is more red when there are more alcoholic connections than control connections, and more blue when there are more control connections than alcoholic connections.
